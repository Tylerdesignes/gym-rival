# Rival: Rank Progression & XP Models

To make the 'Rival' app engaging and competitive, here are three ways we can handle rank advancement (Iron to Olympic):

### 1. The XP & Consistency Model (User Favorite)
*   **How it works:** Users earn XP for every action: logging a workout (base XP), hitting a PR (bonus XP), or completing a recommended session.
*   **Why it works:** It rewards consistency. Even if you aren't the strongest, you can reach high ranks by never missing a day. 
*   **Visual:** A progress bar showing "XP until Bronze".

### 2. The Elo / Performance Model (The "Pro" Approach)
*   **How it works:** Similar to competitive gaming. Your rank is based on your performance relative to your "Rival" and your peer group. If you lift more or run faster than the average for your "build," your rank increases.
*   **Why it works:** It's a true measure of 'Rivalry'. You have to actually improve your stats to climb.
*   **Visual:** A "Skill Rating" number that fluctuates.

### 3. The Hybrid Assessment Model (My Recommendation)
*   **How it works:** Rank is a combination of **Consistency (XP)** and **Milestones (Assessments)**. You earn XP to "qualify" for the next rank, but to actually level up (e.g., Silver to Gold), you must pass a "Rank-Up Assessment" (the baseline assessment you mentioned).
*   **Why it works:** It keeps the daily engagement high while ensuring the 'Olympic' rank actually means something.

### Suggested Screens for Development:
1.  **Home Page:** Workout logging, AI-driven recommendations, Rival status, and current rank display.
2.  **Challenger Section:** Direct comparison with your Rival, their daily activity feed, and the "Path to Next Rank" (XP/Tasks).
3.  **Leaderboard:** Global vs. Local (50-mile) toggles, showing the elite Olympic athletes and your local competition.
4.  **Profile:** Growth charts (since install), status, and the 'Baseline Assessment' portal.
